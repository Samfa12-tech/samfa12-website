Privacy

This page explains what information may be collected when you visit Samfa12, join the mailing list, apply to test a game, or follow a link to another platform.

Last updated: July 2026

Your data

What happens to information

Samfa12 aims to collect only the information needed to understand site use, send requested updates, and organise testing opportunities.

Analytics

Microsoft Clarity

Where analytics are enabled, this site uses Microsoft Clarity to understand general activity such as page visits, navigation and interactions. Clarity may use cookies or similar technology and receive technical information including device, browser and approximate location data.

Samfa12 uses these insights to find broken or confusing parts of the site and improve the visitor experience. For details about how Microsoft handles information, read the Microsoft Privacy Statement.

Security and performance

Cloudflare

Samfa12 uses Cloudflare to protect the site, deliver security headers, detect harmful automated traffic, and measure page-loading performance. Cloudflare Web Analytics collects aggregate timing and page-view metrics and is designed not to collect or use visitors' personal data.

Cloudflare may run an invisible browser check or set strictly necessary security cookies when needed to distinguish legitimate visits from malicious traffic. Learn more in Cloudflare's Privacy Policy and cookie documentation.

Signups

Mailing list and testing

Mailing-list and tester signups use Google Forms. The form tells you what it asks for before you submit it. This may include contact details, testing preferences, device information, or feedback you choose to provide.

Samfa12 uses submitted details for the signup you selected: sending relevant project and release updates, arranging test access, and following up on testing feedback. Google processes form data under its own Privacy Policy.

Destinations

External platforms

Samfa12 links to services such as itch.io, Steam, Google Play, Amazon, GitHub, YouTube and social platforms. Those sites may collect information when you visit, sign in, make a purchase or interact with their services.

Their privacy practices and account controls are governed by their own policies. Review the privacy information on the relevant platform if you want to understand or change how it uses your data.

Pocket Audio

Handoff transfer codes

When you choose to create a short Pocket Audio handoff code, the PCS1 song data is sent to the Samfa12 handoff relay so another device can retrieve it. The relay is intended for song-transfer data, not names, email addresses, or account details.

Short-code transfers expire automatically after about 30 minutes. If you prefer not to use the relay, the handoff tool also provides local copy, link, and file-download options.

Control

Retention, removal and contact

Samfa12 keeps signup details while they are useful for the mailing list, testing programme, related follow-up, or basic record-keeping. Information held by Microsoft, Google or another external platform may be kept according to that provider's own policy.

You can ask Samfa12 to stop sending updates, remove you from a tester list, or delete signup details held by Samfa12. Use the unsubscribe option in an update where available, reply to a message you have received, or contact Samfa12 through an official channel on the Links page.

Include enough information to identify the signup and the request. Samfa12 will review it and respond when possible; requests involving data held by an external provider may need to be made directly to that provider.
